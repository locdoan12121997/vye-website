[tg_accordion title="Day 1 August 24th: Resilient Team" icon="plus" close="1"]During this one-day of intensive training, participants would be able to engage in the workshops and activities to be more effective in understanding themselves and how to look for the right partner for their startup. Everyone mentions that cofounding a venture is like marriage which produce 1+1=3 effect. There are 2 facets in the problem of finding the right co-founder: understand thyself and what kind of partner you need. Definitely the process of finding a co-founder would be very different from finding a life partner as the challenge you will encounter with your co-founder would be very different. It would be very helpful to listen of entrepreneurs who successfully found their partners in running the business. This skill would be expanded as you will build up the team over time. The ability to scale up the team from 5 people to 100 people is so important and vital for the future entrepreneur to establish as of now. Mistake in building the initial team and culture will be amplified when your venture grows. Without prior experience in this, it will become unmanageable. There are a lot of myths into building the right team: "Get the A person on the bus", "What we all care about when investing in startup is THE TEAM". Let us decode this for you![/tg_accordion] [tg_accordion title="Day 2 August 25th: Innovative Product" icon="plus" close="1"]Building a startup is hard because it requires the fight with the current establishment to discover. It is challenging, brutal and lots of sweat and tears. However, it is the work of your mission to solve the problems of customers, your strong belief in what is supposed to be done. Some products become loved by customers while some products become hatred. Some product are profitable and some product are not. What exactly defines the product? How to navigate effectively the path of sustainable and market-viable innovation. On the other hand, the product is the well mix of many factors: what you sell, what is the experience customers get from your company, what is your long-term engagement with the customers who trusted in you. We also try to bring you this big picture of how different startups approach the same problem with different solutions via the case study discussion in the masterclass format so that you can reuse the same knowledge for your venture. Let's get on the journey to build the next product which customers love![/tg_accordion] [tg_accordion title="Day 3 August 26th: Compelling Sale Pitch" icon="plus" close="1"]In the startup world, product & sale come hands in hands. It would be costly to you to build the product without clear market validation. If you are not able to project your product well to the customer, you will fail to gain the insights in building the right product. The challenging part of making your great sale pitch is that you need to sell the future of your company. You need to engage the users when you don't have anything at hands yet the future and the imagination you can sketch out. This takes an artist with strong communication skill to engage all the stakeholder with such a vision. People who are selling innovation like Steve Jobs and Jack Ma all have such a personal charm in the way they craft out their sale pitch to investors, customers and employees.
